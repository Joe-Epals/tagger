Welcome

Welcome to the Learning Resource Metadata Initiative (LRMI) Tagger Version 1.0. The LRMI Tagger is a metadata-tagging tool that is designed to support tagging of both individual and bulk processing of educational resources. This user guide provides you with detailed instruction on the use of this tool.

Following is overview information to provide context to how Tagger fits into the Shared Learning Infrastructure.

The Learning Resource Metadata Initiative is a joint venture between Creative Commons and the Association of Educational Publishers to drive the creation and adoption of a metadata schema to consistently describe educational resources.

The Shared Learning Collaborative is a state led initiative funded by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation and the Carnegie Corporation designed to develop internet scale infrastructure to make personalized learning a reality for every US student by improving the usefulness, variety and affordability of education technology.

The Learning Registry is joint effort of the Department of Education and Department of Defense to create a system that is designed to share metadata about learning resources, ratings, reviews, comments and annotations, usage information and information about relationships between resources.

It is worth noting that you may be best served to aggregate your content for tagging before actually using Tagger. Our experience during the proof of concept with publishers has been that capturing the required metadata across all of the resource you intend to tag with LRMI content saved a great deal of time during the tagging process.

This user guide applies to the 1.0 release of Tagger. This version of Tagger works in Google Chrome on Mac and Windows. Future releases will be tested across additional browser and OS combinations.
