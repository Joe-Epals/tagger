Welcome

Welcome to inBloom Tagger Version 1.1 inBloom Tagger is a metadata-tagging tool that is designed to support both individual tagging and bulk processing of educational resources. This user guide provides you with detailed instruction on the use of this tool.

About inBloom

inBloom is a state led initiative funded by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation and the Carnegie Corporation designed to develop internet scale infrastructure to make personalized learning a reality for every US student by improving the usefulness, variety and affordability of education technology.

Related Efforts

inBloom is working in parallel with other important efforts, including the Learning Resources Metadata Initiative (LRMI) and the Learning Registry, to ensure metadata standards are in place that allow the vision of standard-aligned educational content to be achieved in a consistent manner across all applications and uses.

The Learning Resource Metadata Initiative is a joint venture between Creative Commons and the Association of Educational Publishers to drive the creation and adoption of a metadata schema to consistently describe educational resources.

The Shared Learning Collaborative is a state led initiative funded by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation and the Carnegie Corporation designed to develop internet scale infrastructure to make personalized learning a reality for every US student by improving the usefulness, variety, and affordability of education technology.

The Learning Registry (LR) is joint effort of the Department of Education and Department of Defense to create a system that is designed to share metadata about learning resources, ratings, reviews, comments and annotations, usage information and information, about relationships between resources.

Before You Begin

It is recommended that you aggregate your content for tagging before beginning to use inBloom Tagger. Our experience during the proof of concept with publishers has been that capturing the required metadata across all of the resources you intend to tag with LRMI content saved a great deal of time during the tagging process.

About this Guide

This user guide applies to the 1.0.2 release of inBloom Tagger. This version of inBloom Tagger supports Google Chrome on Mac and Windows. Future releases will be tested across additional browser and OS combinations.
